About This Project

Public transportation is not distributed equitably. This project compares demographic vulnerability—including racial minority populations, poverty rates, and senior citizen concentrations—against transit network reach in New York City and Washington, D.C. The choropleth maps show where high-need communities live, overlaid with the actual transit network. The bar charts measure the disparity in average distance to the nearest transit stop between minority-majority and non-minority neighborhoods. Together, these visualizations reveal where future transit investment would have the greatest equity impact.
